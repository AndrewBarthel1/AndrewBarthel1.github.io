Location

Range: Front

Elevation: 10,100 ft

Cordinates: 39°59'41"N 105°38'03"W

Road Difficulty

A rating of 2/5 means an unpaved road that is primarily dirt, but features some unavoidable rocks.

Nessesary Clearance: 5 inches

Other Info

Parking: Room for 20 cars

Campsite Ground Hardness: Decent, but prone to wetness. Center stand recommended.

Bathrooms: 2 in parking lot

Description

The Forth of July Trailhead is a classic for front range hiking and scrambling. About 2 hours west of Denver, the trailhead takes you to the Indian Peaks Wilderness Area and Medicine Bow Wilderness Area. Getting up the trailhead on a bike isn't too difficult with a standard ADV bike, and a dirt bike would wipe the floor with this trail. 5 inches of clearance is reccomended, but with good path finding, is not needed so long as it is later in the summer. Inclines are up to 25 degrees, but only for short sections. The trail is too thin for cars to pass super comfertably, but bikes can certainly get around, and paths are prevelent enough for most parts of the road to be ridable. South and North Arapahoe peaks are both class 2 during the summer, with a traverse that gets up to class 4.
